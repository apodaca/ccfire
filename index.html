  • 🌤️ Burn only during daylight hours and monitor the weather
  • 🔥 Stay with the fire until it is completely out
  • 📞 Call dispatch again when the burn is complete

🔥 Understanding Fire Weather Hazards

Fire behavior is heavily influenced by weather conditions. Here's how each key factor contributes to fire risk:

  • 🌡️ High Temperatures: Hot air dries out vegetation and makes it easier for fires to ignite and spread.
  • 💧 Low Relative Humidity (RH): When RH drops below 20%, fuels like grass and brush dry quickly, making them more flammable.
  • 💨 Strong Winds: Wind can quickly fan flames and push fire fronts across long distances. Gusty conditions are especially dangerous.
  • ⚡ Dry Lightning: Thunderstorms that produce lightning but little rain can ignite wildfires in dry conditions.
  • 🌿 Fuel Dryness: Vegetation becomes fuel. When it's dry—especially in spring and late summer—it's more likely to ignite and carry fire.
  • 🌵 Drought: Extended dry periods lower soil and fuel moisture, setting the stage for more intense and unpredictable fires.
